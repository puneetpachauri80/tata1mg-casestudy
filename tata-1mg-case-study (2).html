02 — What's Strong

Three moats no one can copy.

Moat 01
The Tata Brand

150 years of Indian family trust applied to the highest-stakes consumer category. When a 68-year-old retired teacher sees "Tata 1mg," he's willing to try digital health. That brand equity cannot be bought or replicated.

Moat 02
The Triangle

Pharmacy + diagnostics + teleconsultation in one platform. No competitor combines all three. Apollo 247, Dr. Lal PathLabs, Practo — each owns one vertex. 1mg owns all three. The flywheel exists in strategy, not yet in product.

Moat 03
500M Page Views

More Indians search for health information on 1mg than any competitor. 500 million monthly page views from organic health content — built through years of genuine investment, not ad spend.

03 — What's Broken

Three failures, one root cause.

I walked through the complete purchase journey end-to-end — from home screen to order confirmation — for a chronic medicine user. Three interconnected failures emerged.

Failure 01

Verified patients treated as strangers

Sunita has ordered the same Metformin for 8 months. The app knows her. Yet she re-selects her prescription from the vault every month. She scrolls past 25 home screen sections to find Previously Ordered Items. She receives no refill reminder at day 25. The platform has 8 months of her health data and uses none of it.

Failure 02

The flywheel doesn't exist in the product

Sunita has been on Metformin for 8 months and 1mg has never suggested she check her HbA1c (recommended quarterly for diabetics). When she does book a test and receives her result — 7.8% — the report is a PDF with numbers and reference ranges. No plain-language interpretation. The pharmacy knows she's diabetic. The diagnostics team doesn't. They share a user, an app, and a brand — they share no health intelligence.

Failure 03

Monetisation is adversarial

The Care Plan modal blocks the cart. The handling charge appears at the last step. Paid placements mix with editorial without disclosure. The locked coupon shows discounts the user can never reach. On a health platform — where trust is the primary value proposition — adversarial monetisation is self-defeating.

04 — The Recommended Design

The health flywheel.

The single highest-leverage product investment: connecting pharmacy, diagnostics, and consultation into a continuous loop where every step drives engagement in the next vertical, generates revenue, and improves health outcomes.

💊

Step 1 — Medicine-to-Test Intelligence

A clinical rules engine maps every chronic medicine to its recommended monitoring tests. Metformin → HbA1c quarterly, kidney function annually. When Sunita orders Metformin and her last HbA1c was 4 months ago: "Your HbA1c is overdue — book now from ₹299, home collection tomorrow." One prompt. No nagging. Clinically appropriate.

📊

Step 2 — Test-to-Insight Intelligence

When Sunita receives her HbA1c result — 7.8% — the app shows plain-language interpretation: "Your HbA1c is above target but improved from 8.4%. Your management is working. Continue Metformin. Retest in 3 months." The test result becomes an action plan — not a PDF dump.

👨‍⚕️

Step 3 — Consultation Continuity

When Sunita books a teleconsultation, the doctor sees her medicine history, lab results, and previous notes before calling. After the call: "Dr. Sharma's care plan: continue Metformin, retest HbA1c in 3 months, walk 20 minutes after dinner." Every recommendation is an actionable platform interaction.

07 — Why This Solution

Why this bet over everything else.

Reason 01
It Serves Health

A diabetic who never misses an HbA1c test because 1mg reminds her proactively is a patient whose diabetes is better managed. Her HbA1c improves. Her complications reduce. The primary purpose is health outcomes.

Reason 02
It Compounds Revenue

Every pharmacy order creates a diagnostics opportunity (₹299). Every result creates a consultation. Every consultation creates a prescription renewal. Revenue per user doesn't grow 1× — it grows 3×.

Reason 03
It Creates Lock-In

A user with 3 years of HbA1c trends, whose monitoring schedule is managed by the platform, whose doctor has her complete history — switching means starting from zero. The deepest retention is genuine health value.

08 — Rollout

Eighteen months to health companion.

M 1–2

Clinical rules engine Design

Map top 50 chronic medicines to recommended monitoring tests. Medical advisory review. Data model for patient health timeline.

M 3–5

Medicine-to-test integration Engineering

Prompting engine on order confirmation. WhatsApp refill reminders. Smart home screen for returning patients.

M 6–8

Intelligent result interpretation Engineering

Plain-language test results with historical trends. Teleconsultation CTA on abnormal results. Calendar integration for retest reminders.

M 9–10

5% experiment — 2 cities A/B Test

One tier-1 + one tier-2 city. Matched control group. Measure cross-vertical rate, retention, and health adherence metrics.

M 11–14

Consultation continuity Engineering

Doctor sees full patient history pre-call. Post-consultation care plans with actionable 1mg links. Family health dashboard.

M 15–18

National scale Scale

25% → national rollout if guardrails hold. Checkout de-friction. Adversarial monetisation cleanup. Health companion identity.

09 — The Big Picture

From marketplace to health companion.

The marketplace version of 1mg is 25 sections of products on a home screen. The companion version knows your name, your condition, your medicines, your test schedule, your family's health — and greets you Monday morning with:

"Your Metformin is due Thursday. Your HbA1c test is in 2 weeks. Your mother's prescription expires April 30. Here's your family health plan for the week."

That product is buildable — not in 5 years, but in 18 months — with focused investment in the flywheel, the intelligence layer, and the family health management that makes every interaction genuinely about the user's health rather than about completing a transaction.

1mg isn't a broken product. It's an incomplete one — and the distance between what it is and what it could be is exactly the kind of gap that makes product work worth doing.
